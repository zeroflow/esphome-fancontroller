Your board ships with factory firmware already flashed. This guide walks you from unboxing to a working fan controller in Home Assistant, covering WiFi provisioning, fan wiring, and Home Assistant adoption.

<Aside type="note" title="Already flashed from the factory">
Every WiFi Fan Controller is flashed with factory firmware before shipping. First-time users can skip straight to WiFi provisioning below. If you ever need to re-flash (corruption, recovery, or a custom ESPHome build), the "Re-flash firmware" section at the bottom of this page covers it.
</Aside>

## What You'll Need

- WiFi Fan Controller board
- USB-C data cable (not charge-only), used for serial-based WiFi provisioning
- 12V DC power supply (5.5x2.1mm barrel jack)
- One or more PWM fans (4-pin)

## Setup Steps

<Steps>

1. **Connect to WiFi**

   Connect the board to your computer with a USB-C data cable.

   Open a tool that speaks Improv Serial (for example [improv-wifi.com/serial/](https://www.improv-wifi.com/serial/) in Chrome or Edge), or open a serial console connected to the board.

   Enter your **2.4 GHz** WiFi network name and password. 5 GHz networks are not supported by any board revision.

   Once connected, the board displays its IP address in the serial output.

   <Aside type="note" title="Fallback hotspot">
   If WiFi connection fails, the board creates a fallback hotspot called **"Fan Controller Fallback"**. Connect to this hotspot from your phone or laptop to reconfigure WiFi credentials.
   </Aside>

2. **Connect your fans**

   - Connect your **12V DC power supply** to the board's power input (5.5x2.1mm barrel jack)
   - Plug your **PWM fans** (4-pin) into the fan headers (Fan 1 through Fan 4)
   - Check your hardware revision page for the correct pin layout and connector orientation

   <Aside type="caution" title="USB-C does not power fans">
   The USB-C port is for flashing and serial communication only. Fans require 12V DC power through the barrel jack connector. The USB-C port provides only 5V to the ESP32 microcontroller.
   </Aside>

3. **Adopt in Home Assistant**

   Your fan controller is automatically discovered by Home Assistant via the ESPHome integration.

   - **If you have the ESPHome add-on:** The device appears in your ESPHome Dashboard. Click **Adopt** to add it.
   - **If you don't have the ESPHome add-on:** Go to **Settings > Devices & Services > Integrations**. The fan controller appears under **Discovered** devices. Click **Configure** to add it.

   Once adopted, your fan controller's entities (fans, temperature, humidity, RPM sensors) appear in Home Assistant ready to use.

   See [Home Assistant Integration](/getting-started/home-assistant/) for dashboard setup and automation examples.

</Steps>

## Re-flash firmware (optional)

You only need this section if:

- The factory firmware was corrupted (board will not boot or is not discovered on a fresh network)
- You want to reset to a known-good factory image
- You are installing a custom ESPHome build you compiled yourself

Most customers never need to re-flash. For routine firmware updates, use OTA through the [ESPHome Dashboard](/getting-started/firmware-updates/). No cables needed.

Re-flashing uses the Web Serial API and requires a Chrome or Edge browser plus a USB-C data cable.

<Aside type="caution" title="USB not detected? (ESP32-S2)">
The ESP32-S2 (Rev 2.0+) has known USB reliability issues on some systems. Try these steps:

- Use a different USB-C cable (must be a **data** cable, not charge-only)
- Hold the **BOOT** button while plugging in the USB cable
- Try a different USB port (avoid USB hubs)
- Use [ESPWEBTOOL](https://esptool.spacehuhn.com/) as an alternative flash tool
</Aside>

After re-flashing, repeat the WiFi provisioning step above, then re-adopt in Home Assistant. See [Firmware Updates](/getting-started/firmware-updates/) for OTA updates.
